The Internet Doesn't
Respect Us

The internet’s architecture requires surveillance by design, and every move you make is permanent. Even VPNs just shift the trust problem. Someone always knows.

02

It’s Time For
Some Change

Reticulum doesn’t just fix the internet. It changes it completely. Your entire footprint is a cryptographic key, not an IP address, no ISP involvement, just math. Meanwhile, packets in the network carry zero source information.

03

Encryption Isn’t
Optional

Unencrypted communication over Reticulum isn’t possible. Stripping the encryption breaks the routing, as the entropy from the encrypted packets is the routing logic. No opt-out.

04

Identity Without Authority

Your identity is a cryptographic key you generate yourself. No registration, no database, no authority. Portable across any medium, your identity follows you — only when you want.

Generate as many as you want, compromising one reveals nothing about the others. Completely independent.

05

Reticulum is transport-agnostic. The same protocol, the same networks, the same identity — over fiber, Bluetooth, LoRa radio, WiFi, and more. A solar-powered field radio talks to a fiber server.

06

How Peer Discovery Works

There’s no DNS or server lookup. Instead, peers in a network announce themselves, broadcasting a proof-of-life for their cryptographic identity.

The announce travels out, allowing peers to record the path back, continuously optimizing the best routes. No coordination required.

07

Application Layer
— LXMF

Reticulum is the network layer — a cryptographic identity, secure transport, anonymous pathing. But that doesn’t support messaging, calling, or file sharing.

That’s where LXMF comes in. The Lightweight Extensible Message Format is an application-layer protocol enabling end-to-end encryption on top of Reticulum’s security.

08

Offline Messages

Without a server or database, messages can’t float in the cloud until delivery — but propagation nodes allow messages sent over the network to be held until a recipient is back online.

This meets modern messaging needs, without compromising on privacy or fundamental principles.
