A letter to our community:

Building For
A Shared Future

There is a particular kind of silence that exists between two devices when they first find each other. Not the silence of nothing happening, but the silence of everything working exactly as it should. A private line, opened. Communication that belongs to nobody else. Today, that silence no longer belongs to you. We believe it should.

Ratspeak started with frustration. Frustration at watching communication infrastructure turn into surveillance infrastructure. Frustration that a conversation between two people no longer stays between two people. Frustration at the assumption that privacy is a feature you subscribe to, rather than a right you are born with.

We looked at the landscape of messaging apps and saw tools that were good but incomplete. Apps that promised privacy but still required servers, phone numbers, accounts, and identity brokers. Systems that could be shut down with a single disaster or corporate acquisition. The reality is, this isn’t solvable at just the application layer.

The world doesn’t lack another messaging app, it lacks honest messaging infrastructure. One that can’t be co-opted. Infrastructure that works when the internet does not. Infrastructure that doesn’t treat its participants as products.

We need something that can’t be turned off, because there is nothing to turn off.

Reticulum gave us the foundation: a networking stack designed from the ground up for autonomy. No dependency on the traditional internet. No assumption of working infrastructure. Just one radical idea that any two devices should be able to find each other and talk securely, whether they are separated by a wall or an ocean. Through radio waves or wires.

We took that foundation and asked: what would it look like if we built a complete ecosystem on top of it? Something that puts the power of Reticulum in the hands of the user, without all the technical jargon. A system that just works, one that is equally convenient.

Most of what we are building is invisible by design. The best infrastructure always is.

But invisibility is not insignificance. Every single packet relayed is end-to-end encrypted. Every identity is self-sovereign and ungovernable. Every user is a participant, strengthening the network. There is no privilege. No authority.

Privacy should not be a product, but a non-negotiable property.

We are building this in the open because we believe tools that enable our basic human rights should not be kept behind walled gardens. Every line of code is available for scrutiny. Discussion is encouraged. Trust should be verifiable, else it remains hope and faith. A world built on hope and faith is what got us here.

To those of you who have contributed code, raised issues, tested software/firmware, or simply told a friend: you are no longer a user. You are a builder.

This network exists because enough people decided things should be different.

We don’t know what the future holds for Ratspeak. We do not know if it will be adopted by thousands or remain a quiet tool used by a dedicated few. What we do know is that the principles it embodies are not negotiable. Privacy is not a feature. Autonomy is not a premium tier. The ability to communicate without permission is not a privilege to be granted by those in power.

If you have read this far, you are the right kind of curious. The world needs more curiosity, it’s not too late.

Join us.

-- THE BUILDERS
